What to Know Before Visiting Gilgit-Baltistan

Gilgit-Baltistan, nestled among the towering Karakoram, Himalaya, and Hindu Kush ranges, is a paradise for adventurers and culture enthusiasts. Known for its dramatic landscapes, ancient forts, and vibrant local traditions, it offers an unforgettable journey into Pakistan’s northern frontier.

🌄 Top Attractions & Experiences - Skardu Valley – home to serene lakes and mighty peaks like K2 - Deosai National Park – the world’s second-highest plateau, teeming with wildlife - Shangrila Resort – a picturesque retreat by Lower Kachura Lake - Rakaposhi Viewpoint – stunning vistas of the 7,788m Rakaposhi peak - Naltar Valley – famous for its colorful lakes and skiing opportunities - Fairy Meadows – a gateway to Nanga Parbat, the ‘Killer Mountain’

FAQs about Gilgit-Baltistan

Can I get a refund for my tour?

Most tours in Gilgit-Baltistan, such as those to Hunza or Skardu, offer a full refund if canceled at least 48 hours before the start date. Check the specific tour’s cancellation policy when booking to confirm details.

Can I change my travel dates?

Many tour operators in Gilgit-Baltistan allow date changes with at least 7 days’ notice, subject to availability. Contact your tour provider directly to arrange a new travel date.

What is the best time to visit Gilgit-Baltistan?

The best times to visit Gilgit-Baltistan are April to May for spring blossoms and September to October for vibrant autumn colors. Summer (June to August) is ideal for trekking, while winter (December to February) suits skiing in Naltar.

Do you arrange airport transfers?

Yes, airport transfers can be arranged to and from Gilgit or Skardu airports for most tours. Please confirm with your tour operator at the time of booking to ensure availability.
